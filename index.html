About me

PhD candidate in sex chromosome evolution. My interests centre around
how the evolution of sex interplays with speciation and diversification.

Comparative Genomics

We know that the diversity of the natural world is imennse, but so much of this is not only visible on the organism level. Much diversity in nature cannot be seen, but is only apparent from analysing the content and configuration of individual genomes. I am interested in analysing whole genome assemblies of multiple species to understand how structural or non-coding elements contribute to this diversity.

Speciation

The greatest marvel of evolution to my mind, is how individual behaviour can impact speciation. Divergence of sexual species involves some initial barrier to reproduction, and I am interested in how the actions of individual organisms can accumulate into the formation of an entirely new species.

Sex determination

Sex is a central pilar of evolutionary theory, yet can take many forms. Differences in divergence of sexes, in sex-related traits and methods of sex determination can inform our understanding of evolution itself. In particular, sex chromosomes are in many ways anomalous within the genome and genomic evolution, and can guide our knowledge of how the genome evolves and the impact sex has on evolution.

Conservation

The loss of biodiversity is and will have unfounded consequences for us and our planet, and the more we know about the species we share it with, will help us better understand the impact we are having, and how further destruction can be prevented.
